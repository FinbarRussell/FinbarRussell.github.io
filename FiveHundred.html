Game Rules

500 is a trick-taking game similar to Whist.

Betting

Each round, a player places a bet. This determines which suit will become trumps and also sets the number of tricks a team must win in order to gain the relevant points, up to a maximum of 500 for all 10 tricks won with no trumps.

Clubs are worth 60, Spades 70, Diamonds 80, Hearts 90, and No Trumps 100. For each number of tricks over 6, 100+ is added to the bet.

If, after 10 tricks, a team does not achieve their bet number of tricks, the points are deducted from their score instead of being added.

The team that did not win the bet will have 10+ points added for each trick they won, up to a maximum of 10.

Upon reaching 500, a team wins. If a team reaches -500, they lose.

Trumps

Once a bet has been made, the suit called becomes trumps. If no trumps are selected, then no suit will be trumps.

The Joker card is considered a trump card and is the highest card in 500.

Bowers

Jacks have a special role in 500. The Jack of the trump suit becomes the right bower and ranks second highest, below the Joker.

The Jack with the same colour but different suit to trumps becomes the left bower. For example, when Hearts are trumps, the Jack of Diamonds becomes the left bower.

The left bower is the third highest-ranking card.

All other cards are ranked as usual: number cards are beaten by the Queen, which is beaten by the King, which is beaten by the Ace.

Tricks

The winner of a trick is the player who plays the highest card.

The player must follow the first suit played if possible. If they do not have this suit, they may play any card.

If a card is played off-suit, i.e., Spades are played but the player has no Spades and plays a Club, then they cannot win unless it is a trump card.

If a trump is played off-suit, then it will automatically win unless another trump that is higher is played.

In No Trumps, the Joker is the only card that can be played off-suit and still win.
